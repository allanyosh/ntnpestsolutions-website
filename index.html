How to Use

Three steps. That's it.

01

Apply near ant trails

Locate where ants are moving. Place a few drops directly on the trail, or on a piece of foil or plastic for easy cleanup.

02

Allow ants to feed

Ants will drink the bait and carry it back to the nest. Avoid disturbing them — each feeding helps spread the bait deeper.

03

Bait reaches the colony

The bait spreads through the nest. Results are typically seen in 1–3 days. Reapply as needed until activity stops.

How it works.

How NTN Works

01

Targets the source, not just the surface

Most ant killers only eliminate the ants you see. NTN is designed to reach the hidden nest where the problem begins.

02

Built to spread before taking effect

The bait works gradually, allowing ants to carry it back and share it within the colony instead of dying immediately.

03

Colony-wide impact over time

As the bait spreads, more ants are affected beyond the visible trail. Activity typically reduces within 1–3 days.

The Science Behind NTN

01

A slow-acting bait system

NTN uses a low concentration of active ingredient. When ingested, it disrupts the ant's internal systems over time rather than killing instantly.

02

Shared through trophallaxis

Ants naturally share food with one another through a process called trophallaxis. This allows the bait to spread deeper into the colony.

03

Designed to work with ant behaviour

By aligning with how ants feed and interact, the bait reaches areas that surface sprays cannot.

Why Borax Works

01

Slow-acting by design

Borax functions as a stomach poison when consumed, gradually disrupting internal processes.

02

Time enables spread

Because the effect is not immediate, ants continue moving, feeding, and interacting within the colony.

03

Reaches beyond the trail

This allows the active ingredient to spread further than what's visible, increasing overall effectiveness.

Reaching the Queen

01

Workers don't reproduce

The ants you see are typically workers. They forage for food, but the queen is responsible for reproduction.

02

Food flows through the colony

Through trophallaxis, nutrients are shared across the nest, allowing the bait to travel deeper inside.

03

Affecting the source reduces the colony

When the queen is affected, the colony's ability to sustain itself declines, leading to a gradual reduction in activity.

Why NTN

Sprays, gels, and liquid baits can all reduce ant activity. The key difference is how easily the active ingredient moves through the colony.

Why NTN Exists

Made because
nothing worked.

After moving into my new place, ants started invading our home. At first it was just a few, then suddenly they were everywhere. I found them in my pantry, getting into our food, in our water jug, and even in my plants. It started to feel like we couldn't keep anything clean. After enough nudging from my wife to fix it, I tried the usual home remedies and store-bought products, but none of them worked. The ants kept coming back, no matter what I did. So I decided to make my own.

I started experimenting with different ingredients and ratios, testing early versions with friends and family. With each round of feedback, I adjusted and tried again. Over time, it became something that worked reliably. A liquid bait that ants would take back and spread through the colony.

That is how NTN came about.

Borax is a naturally occurring mineral commonly used in cleaning products, cosmetics, and various industrial applications.
Borax has low toxicity to humans when used as directed, but it is effective against small insects like ants when ingested. As a precaution, apply the bait in hard-to-reach areas and avoid placing it where pets or children may come into contact with it.
The slow action is intentional. A fast-kill stops at the surface. Borax works gradually so worker ants have time to carry the bait back to the nest and share it with the colony before it takes effect. This is what allows it to reach the queen and nest.
Most users notice a reduction in ant activity by day 1 to 3. In some cases, results are visible within 24 hours. Larger infestations may take a little longer — reapply as needed until activity stops.
Apply directly on the ant trail. A few drops is enough. For a cleaner setup, place drops on a piece of aluminium foil or a plastic card — easy to remove when done.
Ants won't feed if they're disturbed or not actively foraging. Your shadow or movement near them can stop them. Wait a few hours and try again. Make sure the bait is placed directly on or close to their trail.
Borax is relatively safe, but we recommend applying in areas your pets can't easily access — under furniture, behind appliances, along baseboards. Avoid open areas where pets roam freely.
Dry tissue to remove the bulk, damp tissue to clear the remaining residue. If you used foil or a card, dispose of it directly.
One 30ml bottle handles most infestations — often with multiple applications. Each use only requires a few drops. Larger infestations will use more, but one bottle goes a long way.
